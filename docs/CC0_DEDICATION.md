## Rationale

### Why CC0?

The Narrow Way Project uses CC0 for several reasons:

1. **Maximum Freedom**: We want this project to be useful to as many people as possible, without legal barriers
2. **No License Compliance Burden**: Users don't need to track licenses, include notices, or worry about compatibility
3. **Educational Value**: Students and learners can use and study the code without restrictions
4. **Commercial Friendly**: Businesses can adopt NWP without legal review delays
5. **Public Good**: Development infrastructure tools should be freely available

### Comparison with Other Licenses

| License | Requires Attribution | Copyleft | Patent Grant |
|---------|---------------------|----------|--------------|
| CC0 (Public Domain) | No | No | N/A |
| MIT | Yes | No | No |
| Apache 2.0 | Yes | No | Yes |
| GPL v3 | Yes | Yes | Yes |

CC0 is the most permissive option, removing all restrictions.

## FAQs

### Can I use NWP in a commercial product?
Yes, absolutely. You can use NWP code in commercial products without payment, permission, or attribution.

### Do I have to release my modifications?
No. You can modify NWP and keep your changes private or proprietary.

### Do I have to provide attribution?
No, but it's appreciated. A link back to the NWP repository helps others discover the project.

### Can I relicense NWP under a different license?
Yes. Because NWP is public domain, you can incorporate it into projects under any license you choose (MIT, GPL, proprietary, etc.).

### What if I contribute code?
By contributing, you agree to dedicate your contribution to the public domain under CC0. This keeps the entire project freely available.

### What about patents?
CC0 includes a patent waiver to the extent possible. However, we cannot guarantee freedom from third-party patents.

### Can this dedication be revoked?
No. CC0 dedications are irrevocable. Once in the public domain, the work remains there forever.
