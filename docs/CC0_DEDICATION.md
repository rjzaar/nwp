## Rationale

### Why dual `CC0-1.0 OR MIT`?

The Narrow Way Project uses this composite for several reasons:

1. **Maximum Freedom**: CC0 expresses the author's intent — the work
   is public domain — better than any pure permissive licence.
2. **Jurisdictional Resilience**: Australian law on unilateral
   abandonment of copyright is unsettled. The MIT Licence operates
   as a fallback wherever CC0 fails. The Rust ecosystem's
   `MIT OR Apache-2.0` standard demonstrates that dual-licensing is
   well-understood by tooling.
3. **No Licence Compliance Burden** (CC0 path): Users on the CC0
   path don't need to track licences, include notices, or worry
   about compatibility.
4. **Standard Permissive Fallback** (MIT path): Users in
   jurisdictions where CC0 may not work get a universally-recognised
   permissive licence instead.
5. **Educational Value**: Students and learners can use and study
   the code without restrictions.
6. **Commercial Friendly**: Businesses can adopt NWP without legal
   review delays.

### Comparison with Other Licences

| Licence | Requires Attribution | Copyleft | Patent Grant |
|---------|---------------------|----------|--------------|
| CC0 (Public Domain) | No | No | Implicit (§4) |
| MIT | Yes (per file) | No | No |
| Apache 2.0 | Yes | No | Yes |
| GPL v3 | Yes | Yes | Yes |

The CC0 + MIT composite gives recipients the most permissive option
they can rely on in their jurisdiction.

## FAQs

### Can I use NWP in a commercial product?
Yes, absolutely. You can use NWP code in commercial products without
payment, permission, or attribution under the CC0 path; under the
MIT path you must preserve the copyright notice.

### Do I have to release my modifications?
No. You can modify NWP and keep your changes private or proprietary.

### Do I have to provide attribution?
Under CC0, no. Under the MIT fallback, the copyright notice must be
preserved in copies or substantial portions of the Software. Either
way, attribution is appreciated.

### Can I relicense NWP under a different licence?
Yes. Because NWP is dual-licensed under permissive terms, you can
incorporate it into projects under any compatible licence you choose.

### What if I contribute code?
By contributing, you agree to the inbound = outbound dual licence,
DCO sign-off, s 195AW moral-rights consent, and AI-assistance
disclosure described in [CONTRIBUTING.md](../CONTRIBUTING.md).

### What about patents?
A patent grant is provided in [NOTICE](../NOTICE) §5. CC0's §4(a)
includes a non-assertion covenant; the MIT fallback does not grant
patents; the NOTICE §5 grant covers both paths.

### Can this dedication be revoked?
No. CC0 dedications are irrevocable. The MIT Licence is also
perpetual and irrevocable for past versions. Future versions of the
project remain freely available.
